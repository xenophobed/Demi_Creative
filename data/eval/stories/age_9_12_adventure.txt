The elevator in Ava's apartment building had always gone from floors one to fifteen. But on Tuesday, a new button appeared: floor negative one. She pressed it. The doors opened to a vast underground market lit by bioluminescent mushrooms. Vendors sold bottled echoes, maps of dreams, and keys that opened doors that did not exist yet. A woman with silver eyes handed her a compass that pointed not north, but toward "the thing you need most." The needle spun wildly before settling on a direction — straight back up. Ava understood. Sometimes the greatest adventure is realizing you already have what you need.
