The astronomers called it the Quiet Comet because it produced no tail, no dust, no spectacle at all. But twelve-year-old Priya noticed something they missed. On the night it passed closest to Earth, she pointed her homemade radio telescope at it and heard music — not static, not interference, but a slow, haunting melody that rose and fell like breathing. She recorded forty-seven seconds before the signal faded. Her professor said it was atmospheric noise. Priya kept the recording anyway. Some nights, when she could not sleep, she played it back and wondered who, somewhere out in the dark, was singing.
