There is a theory that every person has a frequency — a specific note that, if you could hear it, would sound like the truest version of who they are. Leo's grandmother used to hum the same note every evening while she washed the dishes: a low, steady B-flat. She said she learned it from her mother, who learned it from hers. After his grandmother passed, Leo sat at her piano and pressed the B-flat key. The note hung in the air like it had been waiting for him. He kept his finger on the key until the sound faded, and then played it again. He would learn his own note eventually. For now, hers was enough.
